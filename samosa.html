Samosa Recipe

Deliciously crisp samosas, perfect for any occasion

Preparation Ingredients Steps Nutrition

Preparation Time & Servings

Preparation Time: 45 minutes

Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Water, as needed
  • 1 teaspoon carom seeds (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps

Step 1: In a large bowl, mix the all-purpose flour, oil, salt, and carom seeds (if using). Gradually add water, a little at a time, and knead the mixture into a firm dough. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes while you prepare the filling.

Step 2: Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and allow them to sizzle for a few seconds. Then, add the minced ginger and saut√© for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

Step 3: Add the boiled and mashed potatoes, green peas, coriander powder, garam masala, chili powder, and salt. Stir everything together and cook for about 2-3 minutes to let the spices mix evenly. Turn off the heat and add chopped cilantro. Allow the filling to cool completely before using it to fill the samosas.

Step 4: Once the dough has rested, divide it into equal-sized small balls. Roll each ball into a circle (about 6 inches in diameter). Cut each circle in half to form two semi-circles.

Step 5: Take one semi-circle, fold it into a cone shape, sealing the edges with a little water. Fill the cone with about 1-2 tablespoons of the potato-pea filling, then seal the top edge by pressing the sides together with water.

Step 6: Heat oil for deep frying in a pan over medium heat. Carefully slide the samosas into the hot oil and fry them until they are golden brown and crispy, turning occasionally for even frying. Remove and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

Step 7: Serve the hot, crispy samosas with your favorite chutney or dipping sauce. Enjoy!
